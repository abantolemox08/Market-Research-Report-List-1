<p><p>Portable gas detectors utilize various technologies to monitor and detect hazardous gases in real-time. Electrochemical technology involves sensors that react with specific gases, producing measurable electrical signals. Semiconductor technology uses solid-state sensors that change resistance in the presence of gases, providing rapid detection. Infrared technology measures gas concentrations by analyzing absorbed infrared light, ideal for hydrocarbon detection. Other market types may include photoionization detectors and catalytic bead technology, catering to diverse applications and environmental conditions.</p></p>

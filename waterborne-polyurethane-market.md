<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Waterborne Polyurethane Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anionic PUDs</li><li>Cationic PUDs</li><li>Non-Ionic PUDs</li><li>Component Polyurethane</li><li>Two-component Polyurethane</li><li>Urethane-modified</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Waterborne polyurethane (PUD) dispersions are categorized based on their ionic nature and formulation. **Anionic PUDs** use negatively charged particles, offering stability and flexibility. **Cationic PUDs** rely on positive charges, ideal for adhesion and coatings. **Non-ionic PUDs** lack charges, providing excellent water resistance. **Component polyurethane** systems are single-pack, pre-mixed solutions. **Two-component polyurethane** involves mixing resin and hardener for enhanced durability. **Urethane-modified** products blend polyurethane with other polymers, improving performance. Each type caters to specific applications like coatings, adhesives, and textiles, balancing eco-friendliness and functionality.</p></p>

<p><p>A desiccant dehumidifier is a type of moisture removal device that uses desiccant materials to absorb humidity from the air. Unlike traditional refrigerant dehumidifiers that cool air to condense moisture, desiccant dehumidifiers draw in air and pass it over desiccant materials, such as silica gel or zeolite, which absorb moisture effectively. <p><p>The desiccant dehumidifier market consists primarily of two types: tower type and rotor type. Tower type dehumidifiers use a stationary desiccant material housed in a vertical configuration, where moist air passes through for moisture absorption. Conversely, rotor type dehumidifiers utilize a rotating wheel filled with desiccant material that continuously absorbs moisture while cycling through regeneration zones to release captured water. Both types are effective in maintaining low humidity levels, aiding in various applications such as industrial processes and climate control.</p></p>

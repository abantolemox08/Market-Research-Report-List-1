<p><p>The inorganic binder market comprises several types, including silicate, phosphate, oxides, sulfate, and borates, each serving distinct applications. Silicates offer durability and resistance in construction, while phosphates are utilized for their adhesive properties in ceramics. Oxides, such as alumina, enhance strength in various materials. Sulfates, often used in cement, improve performance and setting times. Borates contribute to fire resistance and chemical stability in glass and ceramics. Collectively, these binders play crucial roles across diverse industrial sectors.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>CIP Method</li><li>Vibration Molding Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Isomolded graphite is produced using two primary methods: the CIP (Cold Isostatic Pressing) method and the vibration molding method. The CIP method involves applying uniform pressure on the graphite mixture while submerged in a fluid, resulting in dense and uniform material. In contrast, the vibration molding method involves placing the graphite mixture into a mold and applying vibrations to compact the material, enhancing its density and uniformity. Both methods contribute to high-performance graphite products used in various applications, including industrial and electronic sectors.</p></p>
